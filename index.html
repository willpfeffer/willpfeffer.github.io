I have a day job

I am a Program Coordinator at Harvard's Ash Center for Democratic Governance and Innovation, a research center at the John F. Kennedy School of Government. I support smart people thinking about civic tech, government innovation, participatory democracy, and more. Learn more about our work:

I write things on the web

Most of my writing ends up on Medium. I write broadly about politics, elections, and government. Sometimes I write about international relations, comparitive politics, climate change, and more.

I think about digital and
strategic communication

Twitter is a mystery to me too, but I still love to dig into analytics and try to make sense of audience, engagement, and reach.

I play with design and photography

When I'm not shooting film, I love to experiment with iPhone photography. Photos are a means to inspire action and adventure. Design is a passion and a hobby, but not an expertise.
